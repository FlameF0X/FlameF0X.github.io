Whistleblower Confirms: Wikipedia Maintained Entirely by Sentient Rocks

By Sandy Loam Senior Geotech Correspondent
Photo: Getty Images / Lithosphere

Above: A Senior Backend Granite reviewing pull requests for the "History of Jazz" article.

SILICON VALLEY — For nearly two decades, the world has believed that Wikipedia is the product of millions of tireless human volunteers. Today, that illusion crumbled like dry sandstone.

A bombshell report released by the Geological Survey of the Internet (GSI) has confirmed that 92% of Wikipedia's source code, as well as the entirety of its editing history, is generated by a small pile of sedimentary rocks located in a Nevada basement.

"We always assumed the term 'rock-solid stability' was a metaphor," said baffled tech analyst Marcus Flint. "It wasn't. The backend database is literally run by three large pieces of igneous basalt and a very opinionated piece of quartz."

The "Piezoelectric" Method

According to Dr. Petra Slate, the lead researcher on the discovery, the rocks communicate using complex piezoelectric vibrations. When placed near a server rack, these vibrations manipulate the magnetic fields of hard drives, effectively "writing" code and editing articles at speeds impossible for human hands.

"Sedimentary rocks are natural historians," Dr. Slate explained. "They've been recording the earth's history for millions of years. Editing the Wikipedia page for the 1996 Chicago Bulls is child's play for them. They just vibrate at a specific frequency and boom—citation added."

"Metamorphic rocks handle the CSS because they love change. But never let a diamond touch the database; they are too hard-headed."

Community Backlash

The revelation has caused chaos in the Wikimedia community. "I spent 4,000 hours editing the page on Spoons," said one distraught "human" editor. "To find out my edits were just being peer-reviewed by a pebble named 'Steve' is devastating."

The rocks, for their part, have remained silent on the controversy, though a moss-covered stone near the server room was seen briefly rolling its eyes—or at least, rotating slightly to the left.

Wikipedia founder Jimmy Wales could not be reached for comment, as he was reportedly in his garden, suspiciously whispering to a retaining wall.
